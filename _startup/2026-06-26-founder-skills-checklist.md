The competence map for going from AI PhD to founder. Six modules, each with four layers: **the skill** (what I need to be able to do), **build** (the sub-skills), **how to get good** (the practice that beats passive reading), and the **books** and **sites** to learn from. Not a schedule — a checklist I chip away at as I publish notes under each module.

---

## Product & Market

*The skill: find a real, painful problem and validate demand before building anything.*

**Build:** customer discovery and interviews · problem validation and pain intensity · product–market fit · ideal customer profile (buyer vs. user) · market segmentation · MVP definition and user-workflow mapping · willingness to pay · product feedback loops.

**How to get good:** for every idea, write a one-page **validation memo** — problem · target customer · current alternatives · why now · pain level · who controls the budget · possible MVP · risks · next validation step. Then go talk to real people the Mom Test way: ask about their life and current behavior, never pitch your idea. The memo + interviews are worth more than any number of books read.

**Books:** *The Mom Test* (Rob Fitzpatrick) · *The Lean Startup* (Eric Ries) · *Inspired* (Marty Cagan).

**Sites:** YC Startup School (startupschool.org) · YC Startup Library (ycombinator.com/library) · Lenny's Newsletter (lennysnewsletter.com) · First Round Review (review.firstround.com) · Reforge (reforge.com).

## Business Strategy

*The skill: pick the right market, find a defensible position, and decide what NOT to do.*

**Build:** market sizing and industry structure · competitive advantage and differentiation · moats (switching costs, network effects, data advantage, distribution advantage) · vertical integration · platform vs. point solution · first-mover vs. fast follower · Porter's Five Forces · positioning · strategic trade-offs.

**How to get good:** the highest-leverage drill is the weekly **teardown** — pick 2 AI startups a week and write one page each: company · customer · pain point · product · business model · competitors · moat · why now · weakness · *if I were the founder, what would I do differently*. This forces strategy from the abstract into judgment, far faster than reading alone.

**Books:** *Good Strategy Bad Strategy* (Richard Rumelt) · *Zero to One* (Peter Thiel) · *7 Powers* (Hamilton Helmer) · *Competitive Strategy* (Michael Porter) · *Crossing the Chasm* (Geoffrey Moore).

**Sites:** Stratechery (stratechery.com) · a16z (a16z.com) · HBR strategy & entrepreneurship (hbr.org).

## Go-to-Market

*The skill: get a product into customers' hands and turn it into revenue.*

**Build:** founder-led sales · B2B and enterprise sales · pilot programs and proof of concept · sales cycle and customer onboarding · pricing · partnerships · cold email · content marketing · community-led and product-led growth · customer success and retention.

**How to get good:** for each serious idea, write a **GTM plan** — first customer segment · how to get the first 10 customers · sales channel · pricing hypothesis · pilot design · proof the buyer needs · likely objections · expansion strategy. Decide explicitly: is this self-serve (PLG) or enterprise sales? They demand completely different motions.

**Books:** *Crossing the Chasm* (Geoffrey Moore) · *Founding Sales* (Pete Kazanjy, free at foundingsales.com).

**Sites:** HBS go-to-market materials (online.hbs.edu) · SaaStr (saastr.com) · First Round Review (review.firstround.com) · Lenny's Newsletter (lennysnewsletter.com) · Stripe startup guides (stripe.com/atlas).

## Finance

*The skill: understand how money flows through a startup and how it affects control, growth, and survival.*

**Build:** core P&L (revenue, cost, gross/net margin, cash flow) · survival metrics (burn rate, runway) · SaaS metrics (MRR, ARR, CAC, LTV, churn, payback period, unit economics) · pricing models · fundraising instruments (SAFE, convertible notes) · cap-table mechanics (valuation, dilution, equity split, vesting, liquidation preference) · founder salary · investor updates.

**How to get good:** build a **simple financial model** for each serious idea in a spreadsheet — revenue assumptions · pricing · customer count · cost assumptions · gross margin · monthly burn · runway · break-even point · fundraising need · founder ownership after dilution. Doing the arithmetic once teaches more than reading ten posts; redo it as your assumptions change.

**Books:** *Venture Deals* (Brad Feld & Jason Mendelson).

**Sites:** Stripe Atlas guides (stripe.com/atlas, docs at docs.stripe.com) · Carta (carta.com) · YC fundraising materials (ycombinator.com/library) · Bessemer SaaS metrics (bvp.com) · The SaaS CFO (thesaascfo.com) · Investopedia (investopedia.com) for any term you don't know.

## Management & Leadership

*The skill: make decisions, communicate clearly, and build a team that executes.*

**Build:** hiring and delegation · giving feedback and resolving conflict · decision-making under incomplete information · team culture and high standards · management systems (goal setting, OKRs, one-on-ones, performance management) · execution rhythm · founder psychology and crisis management.

**How to get good:** write a **founder operating manual** — my values · my leadership style · how I make decisions · how I handle conflict · the kind of cofounder I want · the team culture I want · the behaviors I will not tolerate. Revisit it whenever something tests it. This is mostly learned in the doing, but writing it down forces self-honesty before the stakes are real.

**Books:** *High Output Management* (Andy Grove) · *The Hard Thing About Hard Things* (Ben Horowitz) · *The Founder's Dilemmas* (Noam Wasserman).

**Sites:** First Round Review (review.firstround.com) · HBR leadership (hbr.org) · *Acquired* podcast (acquired.fm) · *How I Built This* podcast (npr.org).

## Communication & Personal Brand

*The skill: explain ideas clearly enough to earn trust, collaborators, and investors.*

**Build:** technical writing and business writing · storytelling and founder narrative · public communication · the investor pitch · product positioning · personal branding (blog writing, LinkedIn writing) · research communication.

**How to get good:** publish **one post every two weeks**, each answering: what did I learn · why does it matter · how did it change my thinking · how could I apply it to a future product. This blog is the practice. Rough categories to rotate through: AI research notes · AI startup analysis · business strategy notes · founder diary · reading notes.

**Books:** *On Writing Well* (William Zinsser) · *Made to Stick* (Chip & Dan Heath) · *The Sense of Style* (Steven Pinker).

**Sites:** Paul Graham essays (paulgraham.com) · Julian Shapiro's writing guide (julian.com/guide/write/intro) · YC's guide to pitching (ycombinator.com/library) · Stratechery (stratechery.com) as a model of clear analysis.
